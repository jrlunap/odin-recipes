Morir Soñando juice

Description

Making Morir Soñando ("To Die Dreaming") is more of a technique than just a list of ingredients. Because it combines acidic orange juice with milk, there is a specific order of operations you must follow to prevent the milk from curdling.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups evaporated milk (chilled)
  • 2 ½ cups freshly squeezed orange juice (chilled and strained of pulp)
  • ¾ cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 cups of ice cubes

Steps

  1. Prep the Bases: Ensure both your orange juice and milk are ice-cold before starting.
  2. Sweeten the Milk: In a large pitcher, combine the cold evaporated milk, sugar, and vanilla. Stir vigorously until the sugar is completely dissolved.
  3. Ice it Down: Add the ice cubes to the sweetened milk and stir again. The milk needs to be as cold as possible before it touches the citrus.
  4. The Critical Step: While stirring the milk mixture constantly and vigorously, slowly pour in the orange juice. Stirring helps the ingredients emulsify rather than separate.
  5. Serve Immediately: Pour into glasses and drink right away. If it sits too long, even a perfectly made Morir Soñando can eventually separate.

The "No-Curdle" Secrets

Order Matters: Always add the juice to the milk, never the other way around.

Temperature is Key: If the milk is even slightly warm, it will curdle instantly upon contact with the acid.

Manual Stirring: Avoid using a blender for the final mix; it can create too much foam. A long spoon or whisk is traditional and effective.
